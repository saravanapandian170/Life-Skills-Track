# **Energy Management**

## **1. Activities that make me relax – Calm Quadrant**
* Taking a short nap
* Listening to music
* Going for a walk
* Talking to someone I trust

---

## **2. When do I find myself getting into the Stress Quadrant?**
* When I start a new or difficult task
* When there is a deadline
* When I feel I have too much work at once
* When I make mistakes or feel unprepared

---

## **3. How do I know I am in the Excitement Quadrant?**
* I feel energetic and focused
* I enjoy what I am doing
* Time passes quickly
* I feel motivated to continue and try new things

---

## **4. Brief Paraphrase of “Sleep Is Your Superpower”**
* Lack of sleep harms the body and mind.
* Sleep loss reduces learning, memory, and brain function.
* It weakens the immune system and raises disease risks.
* Poor sleep affects reproductive health.
* Deep sleep is important for brain cleaning and healthy aging.
* Improving sleep can protect long-term health.

---

## **5. Ideas I Can Implement to Sleep Better**
* Sleep and wake up at the same time every day.
* Keep the room cool and dark.
* Avoid screens before sleeping.
* Reduce caffeine in the evening.
* Do light relaxing activities before bed.
* Avoid heavy meals late at night.

---

## **6. Paraphrase of “Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise”**
* Exercise improves focus, attention, and mood.
* It strengthens memory by boosting the hippocampus.
* Regular workouts protect the brain from aging and diseases.
* Aerobic exercise creates long-lasting positive changes in the brain.
* Exercise acts like a long-term investment for better brain health.

---

## **7. Steps I Can Take to Exercise More**
* Start with short daily walks.
* Fix a specific time for exercise each day.
* Do simple home workouts whenever going outside is hard.
* Choose activities I enjoy (cycling, jogging, dancing, etc.).
* Track progress to stay motivated.
* Increase duration slowly each week.
